I have marked the East Wind seat for convenience.</div><div><img src="/media/Negative-Reverie-Update-4122026_3.jpg"/>Photo Credit: A Mysterious, but Attractive Stranger</div><p>For all my guitar nerds: I <em>used</em> to basically double-amp my tone... I used just the pre-amp portion of amplifiers on my Helix LT (along with effects) and ran it into the "Modern" amp stage of my Quilter Toneblock 202 head. BUT, I have since seen the light and changed how I do things. <br/>Now, I have changed all the pre-amps in my patches to actual full amp heads and now I run it into the "FRFR" portion of my Quilter. Then, on the patches themselves, I have split outputs... One output goes right to the Quilter, like I mentioned, and the other one goes to the Helix LT's XLR output with a cab-sim. <br/>What this means for non-guitar people: I can choose a wider and more accurate palette of amplifier sounds, send a good signal straight to the venue staff from my floor unit AND I can edit patches at home on my computer and have them sound <em>basically</em> the same as they do when you're standing in the room with an amplifier!
