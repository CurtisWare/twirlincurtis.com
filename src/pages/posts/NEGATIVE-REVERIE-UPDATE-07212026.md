It's definitely the most challenging part of the story to get right... I want to make sure the big twists are effective and shocking, but I think when you're writing bad guys, you have to make sure that you don't unwittingly endorse their viewpoints, you know? </p><p>Like, imagine you have an evil king who thinks all orcs should be put to death. You don't want to make the evil king's speech so convincing that the reader thinks "yeah, actually.... he's right we SHOULD murder people based on race" or something like that.
