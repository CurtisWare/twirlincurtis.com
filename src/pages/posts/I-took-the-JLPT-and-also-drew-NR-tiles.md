<p>HELLO! It's MONDAY! The day <em>after</em> I always say I'm gonna write a Patreon post. Oh well.</p><p><strong>THE JLPT</strong></p><p>I spent most of last week cramming for the JLPT N5 that took place yesterday. I took 3 more practice tests courtesy of <a href="https://bunpro.jp" target="_blank">bunpro.jp</a> and was feeling pretty good about it! I was consistently scoring above an 80%... but buddy... I was <em>NOT</em> prepared....</p><p>There was some surprise road work in Philly, so my friend and I who were taking it together ending up getting there later than we wanted and had to run full-speed across the Villanova campus to make it on time. I'm not... the best runner in the world, so for the entire first "Vocabulary" portion of the test, I was wheezing and coughing and choking down snot. At the first break, I was able to go spit a big loogie into the toilet and drink some water though, and that pretty much took care of it.</p><p>Let me tell you, though... that <em>listening portion</em> of the test was MONSTROUS. I'm sure if you have any level of actual fluency, it was probably baby-mode, but it was <em>considerably</em> harder than any practice test I have taken up to this point. I really struggled through it and I'm 100% sure I got a good number of the questions wrong. I'm actually kinda suspecting I'm going to fail the whole test based on just bombing the listening portion... which... is really quite disappointing, but I've made my peace with it. The only thing to be done is to study harder, right? Pass or fail, the results mean the same thing: I need to practice my listening comprehension a LOT more.</p><p><strong>FURTHER LANGUAGE NONSENSE</strong></p><p>So, now that I'm on night shift, I'm going to adopt a slow-but reasonable pace of learning new material. I'm gonna shoot for 4 new kanji, 4 new vocab and 2 grammar points every day to add to my various SRS and Anki decks. It doesn't <em>sound</em> like a lot, but over a year or two, that's a LOT of new material. My intention is to start....
